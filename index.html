Project Detail
SAE Aero Design East 2026 · Advanced Class
Hybrid VTOL Tricopter UAV
Overview

This hybrid VTOL tricopter UAV is being developed for the SAE Aero Design East 2026 Advanced Class competition. The aircraft is designed to take off vertically, transition to fixed-wing cruise flight, and deliver a payload to a designated target area.

The design uses a lifting-body fuselage to generate additional lift during cruise, reducing the aerodynamic penalty of the fuselage and improving overall efficiency. A front-mounted tilting motor configuration provides vertical thrust, with the tilt mechanism validated on the Tricopter Test Platform.

Key Specifications

ConfigurationHybrid VTOL Tricopter
Wingspan~800 mm
AUW~1.6 kg
MotorsT-Motor F80 Pro
Flight ControllerTBS Lucid H7 Wing
AirframeBalsa / Aeroply composite
Yaw ControlFront motor tilt mechanism

Design Approach

The lifting-body fuselage cross-section was aerodynamically shaped to generate lift at cruise angles of attack, reducing weight penalty of a conventional box fuselage. FEA validation confirmed a 10% weight reduction from the front-mounted tilt configuration.

The transition sequence — vertical takeoff, forward tilt, fixed-wing cruise, and vertical landing — was modelled in ANSYS and validated through flight testing.

Aerothon 2025 · UAV Competition
Aerothon Quadcopter
Overview

Designed and built for the Aerothon 2025 disaster-response UAV challenge. The quadcopter features an X-frame configuration with a generative-design single-piece PET-G frame, reducing part count while optimising load paths.

Propulsion was sized to achieve a thrust-to-weight ratio of ~4.58 using 1300 KV BLDC motors on a 440 mm wheelbase, ensuring stable hover and responsive handling in confined environments.

Key Specifications

FrameX-frame, 440 mm wheelbase
Motor1300 KV BLDC
TWR~4.58
Frame MaterialPET-G (generative design)
Design ToolFusion 360 Generative
Structural ValidationANSYS FEA

Design Approach

The airframe was designed using Fusion 360's generative design solver with structural constraints derived from expected flight loads, producing a lightweight single-piece frame that eliminated traditional multi-part assembly.

ANSYS structural analysis verified the frame under maximum thrust and landing impact scenarios before fabrication by 3D printing.

SAE Aero Design West 2025 · Micro Class · 11th / 75 Teams
Micro Class Fixed-Wing UAV
Overview

A high-wing, semi-monocoque fixed-wing UAV designed for the Micro Class at SAE Aero Design West 2025 in Van Nuys, California. The aircraft carries a 2-litre liquid payload within a strict 450 W power limit, placing 11th overall out of 75 international teams.

A research paper on the aerodynamic performance of this aircraft was presented at FMFP 2025, Nirma University.

Key Specifications

Span1.312 m
ConfigurationHigh-wing, semi-monocoque
Payload2 L liquid
Power Limit450 W
Competition Result11th / 75 teams
EventSAE Aero Design West 2025, Van Nuys CA

Design Approach

The wing was designed using XFLR5 for airfoil selection and lift distribution optimisation, balancing payload capacity against the strict power constraint. The semi-monocoque fuselage was fabricated using balsa and composite skin for stiffness-to-weight efficiency.

Component sizing and CG management were iterated through multiple design cycles to meet weight and stability requirements simultaneously.

SAE DDC 2024 & 2025 · Regular Class · 1st Overall 2025
Regular Class Fixed-Wing UAV
Overview

A balsa and aeroply fixed-wing UAV developed across two competition cycles for the SAE DDC Regular Class. In 2025 the aircraft placed 1st Overall across 100+ teams at Karpaga Vinayaga College, Chennai. In 2024 the same platform earned 4th Overall and 2nd Best Design Report at SRM University, Chennai.

The design features a box fuselage with a conventional tail, a high-aspect-ratio wing for efficiency, and an integrated 500 g airdrop mechanism.

Key Specifications

Wingspan1.68 m
AUW~10 kg gross
Empty Weight~3.6 kg (down from 4.1 kg)
MotorT-Motor AM670 520 KV
Prop16 × 10
BatteryLiPo 6S 3300 mAh 40C
Airdrop500 g payload
AirframeAeroply + aluminium spar

Design Approach

The 2025 iteration focused on a structured weight-reduction programme: topology optimisation on the fuselage ribs and spar brackets reduced empty weight from 4.1 kg to 3.6 kg — a 12% improvement — without compromising structural margins.

The aluminium main spar was redesigned with CNC-machined clamps and carbon-fibre reinforcement at high-stress wing root stations, validated through three-point bending FEA before fabrication.

SAE DDC · Structural Component
Primary Landing Gear — 10 kg UAV
Overview

A lightweight tricycle landing gear assembly designed for the 10 kg gross-weight regular class UAV. The system uses carbon-fibre rods as the primary struts, with CNC-machined aluminium clamps for fuselage attachment.

An expanding spring mechanism absorbs landing impact energy, protecting the airframe from shock loads during hard landings on uneven surfaces.

Key Specifications

ConfigurationTricycle
Assembly Weight~250 g
Height235 mm
Gross Aircraft Weight10 kg
Main StrutsCarbon-fibre rods
ClampsCNC-machined aluminium
MechanismExpanding spring impact absorption

Design Approach

Impact load cases were calculated from expected sink-rate and aircraft weight, driving strut sizing and spring selection. ANSYS static structural analysis validated the carbon-fibre strut cross-section under 3g landing loads.

The CNC aluminium clamps were designed with a press-fit interface to the carbon rods, eliminating adhesive joints that had failed in the previous iteration.

Internal Test Platform · Mechanism Validation
Tricopter UAV Test Platform
Overview

A compact tricopter UAV built to validate the servo-actuated rear motor tilt mechanism intended for the Hybrid VTOL design. Rapid-fabricated using laser-cut interlocking MDF tab-and-slot joints, the platform allowed iterative flight testing without committing to expensive materials.

The rear motor tilts via a servo-controlled pivot to provide yaw authority, replacing the need for differential thrust or a conventional tail rotor.

Key Specifications

Wheelbase280 mm
FrameLaser-cut MDF, tab-and-slot
MotorsiFlight XING 2806.5 1300 KV
Flight ControllerCrossflight FC
YawServo-actuated rear motor tilt
PurposeMechanism validation platform

Design Approach

The tilt mechanism was designed with a brass pivot bushing and direct servo linkage to minimise backlash and maximise angular rate. PID tuning was performed iteratively using Mission Planner telemetry logs.

Lessons from this platform — including preferred tilt range, servo authority requirements, and control loop coupling — directly informed the VTOL tricopter design.

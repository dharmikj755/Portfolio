Hybrid VTOL Tricopter UAV

Overview

This hybrid VTOL tricopter UAV is being developed for the SAE Aero Design East competition to combine the vertical takeoff capability of multirotor systems with the aerodynamic efficiency of fixed-wing aircraft. The aircraft is designed to take off vertically, transition into forward flight, and complete the mission using wing-borne lift during cruise. The design focuses on lightweight structural construction, efficient propulsion integration, and reliable transition between VTOL and fixed-wing flight modes.

Key Specifications
ConfigurationHybrid VTOL Tricopter Fixed-Wing UAV
Wingspan800 mm
All-Up Weight~1.6 kg
PropulsionT-Motor F80 Pro motors with 6040 two-blade propellers
Estimated Lift~19 N at 16 m/s airflow
Flight ControllerTBS Lucid H7 Wing
Airframe StructureLifting-body fuselage with two forward-tilting motors
StatusIn Development
Design Approach

The design began with a conventional fixed-wing aircraft with tilting motors mounted on the wings for VTOL capability. Later iterations explored a blended wing–fuselage configuration, but the added structural complexity increased overall weight and reduced payload capacity.

The final design evolved into a lifting-body fuselage with an integrated wing structure and forward-mounted tilting motors. Relocating the tilt mechanism to the front reduced the overall structural weight by approximately 10% while improving layout efficiency. The structure was evaluated using finite element structural analysis (FEA) and validated through multiple flight tests. The airframe uses balsa, aeroply, carbon-fibre rods, Depron sheets, and 3D-printed PETG components to achieve a lightweight yet robust structure.

Aerothon 2025 · UAV Competition · March – August 2025
Aerothon Quadcopter
Overview

This quadcopter UAV was developed for the Aerothon 2025 UAV competition, focusing on efficient propulsion integration, structural reliability, and stable multirotor flight performance. The platform was designed to achieve a high thrust-to-weight ratio while maintaining a lightweight and mechanically robust airframe. The UAV architecture emphasises balanced mass distribution, efficient motor–propeller selection, and structural durability under thrust and asymmetric loading conditions. The design process incorporated iterative CAD modelling, propulsion analysis, and structural validation to ensure reliable flight performance and mission readiness.

Key Specifications
ConfigurationX-frame Quadcopter
Wheelbase440 mm
Propulsion1300 KV BLDC motors with 7×4.2 inch propellers
Max Thrust per Motor~2.06 kg
Total Max Thrust~8.25 kg
Thrust-to-Weight RatioUp to ~4.58
AirframeGenerative-designed frame hub with integrated arms, additive manufactured in PET-G
Design Approach

The mechanical design started with the idea of building a single integrated quadcopter frame instead of assembling multiple separate parts. This approach led to exploring additive manufacturing, which allows complex geometries while reducing overall weight and part count.

Using generative design in Fusion 360, several frame iterations were created by defining load conditions at the motor mounts and electronics mounting points. Different materials such as PLA, PETG, and Nylon were explored to compare structural performance and manufacturability.

The selected frame design was evaluated through structural analysis in ANSYS, where loads of 40 N thrust (≈4 kg total thrust) and a propeller-induced moment of 100 N·mm were applied at the motor mounts. The final design met the required strength and stiffness criteria while maintaining a lightweight structure.

Overview

This fixed-wing UAV was developed for the SAE ISS Drone Development Challenge (DDC) Regular Class — a national competition requiring high-performance unmanned aircraft capable of carrying payloads under strict dimensional and weight constraints. The design targets a high payload fraction while maintaining structural reliability and aerodynamic efficiency.

The platform integrates a dedicated payload bay and cargo delivery system capable of transporting and releasing payloads in flight. The design balances a lightweight yet durable structure, efficient propulsion integration, and stable handling characteristics required for payload transport and controlled aerial delivery missions.

Key Specifications
ConfigurationConventional fixed-wing UAV
Wingspan1.68 m
Empty Weight~3.6 kg
All-Up Weight~10 kg
Payload Capacity500 g cargo delivery
Payload Bay4 × 4 × 10 in  |  Package: 2 × 2 × 4 in
MotorT-Motor AM670 520 KV
Propeller16 × 10
BatteryLiPo 6S 3300 mAh 40C
AirframeAeroply structure reinforced with aluminium members
Design Approach

A box-type fuselage was adopted to efficiently house avionics, propulsion electronics, and the cuboid payload bay within a structurally rigid envelope. The fuselage skin used aeroply panels reinforced with aluminium members, providing a lightweight yet robust framework.

Wing attachment used an aluminium spar passing through an acrylic wing tube, creating a direct load path between the wings and fuselage. A conventional empennage was selected for stability and straightforward manufacturing. Wings and tail surfaces were built from airfoil-shaped ribs at defined intervals, supported by internal rods and shear webs to preserve aerodynamic shape under load.

Topology optimisation was applied to reduce unnecessary material while retaining load-bearing capability. Through successive design iterations and structural refinements, the airframe weight was reduced from 4.1 kg to approximately 3.6 kg — improving the strength-to-weight ratio while fully meeting the competition’s payload and performance requirements.

SAE ISS Design, Development & Competition (DDC) · Structural Component
Primary Landing Gear — 10 kg UAV
Overview

For the SAE ISS Design, Development & Competition (DDC), the aircraft’s primary landing gear was designed to safely withstand landing loads for an all-up aircraft weight of approximately 10 kg. A tricycle landing gear configuration was selected to provide stable ground handling during taxi, takeoff, and landing operations.

The main landing gear uses an expanding spring-based suspension mechanism, allowing the structure to absorb impact energy during touchdown. This reduces the load transmitted to the fuselage and improves durability during repeated landing cycles. The forward landing gear incorporates a suspension-type configuration to further improve stability and shock absorption during ground operations.

Key Specifications
ConfigurationTricycle Landing Gear System
Total System Weight~250 g
Landing Gear Height235 mm
Design Gross Weight10 kg aircraft weight
Main StructureCarbon-fibre rods with CNC-machined aluminium clamps
Shock AbsorptionExpanding spring mechanism
Mounting SystemDual-clamp assembly with adjustable fuselage mounting
Design Approach

The landing gear was designed with propeller clearance, structural strength, and weight efficiency as the primary considerations. The expanding spring mechanism converts vertical landing force into controlled spring expansion, reducing stress on the fuselage structure.

Early prototypes used 3D-printed PETG components for rapid fabrication of complex shapes. After structural evaluation, the design was upgraded to CNC-machined aluminium clamps, providing a significantly better strength-to-weight ratio and improved durability.

The final assembly uses two aluminium clamps. The first mounts the gear to the fuselage via three bolts with an adjustable configuration for fine-tuning ground handling, and supports one end of the spring mechanism. The second clamp guides the carbon-fibre rod carrying the wheels, limiting excessive bending while allowing controlled deflection on landing impact.

Overview

This tricopter UAV was developed as an early test platform to evaluate the feasibility of a tricopter configuration for mission planning and control validation. The primary goal was to verify the servo-actuated rear motor tilting mechanism, which provides yaw control in tricopter systems.

The airframe was constructed using laser-cut MDF, enabling rapid fabrication and quick assembly for iterative testing. With a 280 mm wheelbase, the platform served as a compact experimental vehicle to study flight stability, control response, and tilt mechanism effectiveness under real-world conditions. Multiple flight tests and tuning sessions were conducted to identify performance limitations and refine system parameters.

Key Specifications
ConfigurationTricopter UAV (test platform)
Wheelbase280 mm
Frame MaterialLaser-cut MDF with interlocking tab-and-slot joints
PropulsioniFlight XING 2806.5 1300 KV Brushless Motors
Flight ControllerCrossflight Flight Controller
Yaw ControlServo-actuated rear motor tilting mechanism
PurposeMechanism validation, flight testing & control tuning
Design Approach

The frame was designed with rapid prototyping and ease of manufacturing as the primary goals. MDF was selected because it could be quickly fabricated using in-house laser cutting, enabling fast iteration throughout the testing phase.

Frame components were designed with interlocking tab-and-slot joints that allow parts to fit together precisely without complex fixtures, reducing assembly time and enabling quick modifications between test sessions.

A servo-actuated tilting mechanism at the rear motor mount provides yaw control by rotating the motor in response to control inputs. The platform was used to validate this mechanism through multiple flight tests and tuning iterations, establishing a performance baseline for the full-scale tricopter VTOL design.
